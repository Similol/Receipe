Ingredients:

  • One Tuber of Yam
  • Motar and Pestle
  • Sliced Vegetables(Preferably Spinach leaves)
  • Tomatoes
  • Red bell peppers
  • Onions
  • Iru(locust beans)
  • Palm Oil
  • Crayfish(grounded)
  • Smoked Fish
  • Chopped Pomo(Cow skin)
  • Beef tripes

Ready??

  • First of all, wash your hands, mortar and pestle.
  • Peel the yam, and slice into small-medium cubes, place in a pot with just enough water(and a bit more)
    and leave to boil for 15-25 minutes, depending on the yam
  • Carefully wash your tomatoes, pepper and onions(measuremnts depends on you, **wink)
    and blend to a smooth puree.
    Don't forget to slice some onions down.
  • Get clean saucepan, place on fire and pour a spoon or two of palm oil(depends on you)
  • Let it heat a little. Add your sliced onions and stir, then add your already blended pepper.
  • Add your Iru, and let it simmer for a minute. Don't forget to keep stiring.
  • Add your meatstock, smoked fish, beef tripes and crayfish.
  • Let it simmer till the sauce is very well fried
  • Add your spinach leaves, don't forget to stir it properly (**Take in all that beauty hehe).
  • Don't overcook your vegetables, a minute or to is enough for your vegetables to get steamed the right way.

Almost done...

  • After 15-25 minutes, like I wrote up there, Place your mortar in a very secure and safe spot.
  • Remove the pieces of yam but do not throw away the water.
  • Now boil water in your kettle(we will need it.)
  • Place a few pieces of Yam and start Pounding. Pounded Yam might be stressful
    but the trick is to make sure every hit counts, so you dont have to pound for too long.
  • Keep putting few pieces in and pound. Don't overcrowd your mortar to avoid lumps.
  • By now, your pounded yam has become more sticky, which mean you are right on track.
  • Brace up and keep pounding, wash your hands thoroughly if you want check if there are lumps.
  • Now, get the water you kept from the yam andpour it gently into your mortar
    Use your pestle to gently(emphasis on gently) hit the pounded yam.
    (I like to call it folding method).
  • When the water gets all absorbed, hit it a few times again. Wash your hands and check the texture, It should be sticky and about to be elastic(this is where the boiling water comes in).
  • Get your boiling water and pour gently into the mortar.
    Pour just a little so your pounded yam doesn't become too soft.
  • Again, gently use your pestle to hit it so it can absorb the water and become elastic.
  • Pound it a litlle, till you are satisfied
  • Get your scooping spoon and dish in a plate

Pounded Yam is enjoyed best hot with efo riro and cold palm wine, (**You know, a little something something)
